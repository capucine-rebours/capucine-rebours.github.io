My research examines the psychological mechanisms linking social identity, 
masking, and mental health outcomes in autistic adults. I work within a 
neurodiversity paradigm and a social model of disability, using mixed methods 
(PLS-SEM, reflexive thematic analysis, conceptual content analysis) and 
participatory frameworks. I am particularly attentive to the experiences of 
autistic women and gender minorities, who remain underrepresented in both 
research and clinical practice.

---

## Research Themes

**Autistic burnout**
Conceptualization, severity measurement, predictors, and recovery. A central 
focus of my work is disentangling autistic burnout from depression, and 
identifying the individual, interpersonal, and structural factors that 
contribute to its onset and maintenance.

**Social camouflaging**
Cognitive and psychosocial predictors of camouflaging, its identity costs, 
and its role as a pathway to autistic burnout. I am interested in extending 
this work to longitudinal and intervention-oriented designs.

**Mental health in autism**
Anxiety, depression, trauma, and their intersection with autistic burnout. 
I pay particular attention to alexithymia, executive functioning, and 
emotional processing as transdiagnostic mechanisms.

**Minority stress and intersectionality**
Internalized stigma, autistic identity, and the compounded vulnerabilities 
faced by autistic gender minorities. I draw on Botha and Frost's minority 
stress model to examine how structural and interpersonal stressors shape 
mental health outcomes.

**Participatory and mixed-methods research**
I am committed to community-engaged research that centers autistic 
perspectives at every stage, from design to dissemination.

## Current Projects

**Validation of the ABSI-Fr**
I am finalizing the French adaptation and psychometric validation of the 
Autistic Burnout Severity Items (Arnold et al., 2023), in collaboration 
with the original authors at the University of New South Wales. This work 
involves translation, pilot testing with autistic community members, and 
full psychometric evaluation.

Autistic adults living in France are warmly invited to 
[participate in the study](https://enquetes.univ-tlse2.fr/index.php/199918?lang=fr).

**Completing manuscripts from doctoral research**
I am currently finalizing several articles from my dissertation:
- A qualitative study on recovery strategies and symptom profiles following 
autistic burnout in French autistic adults
- An exploratory quantitative investigation of environmental and psychosocial 
risk factors for burnout severity
- The full psychometric validation of the ABSI-Fr

**Upcoming: INSAR 2026**
I will be presenting at the International Society for Autism Research Annual 
Meeting (Prague, April 2026): *Exploring Predictors of Autistic Burnout 
Severity in Adults: Individual, Interpersonal, and Structural Factors.*
